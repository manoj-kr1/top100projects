Automatic Plant Watering System

Home Projects About Contact

This system waters plants using a soil moisture sensor, relay, and Arduino Uno.

Components:

  • Arduino Uno
    Arduino is an open-source microcontroller platform used for building electronics projects. In this system, we use the Arduino Uno to read soil moisture levels using a sensor. Based on the sensor reading, it controls a relay to turn a water pump on or off, watering the plant automatically. This setup helps in maintaining plant health without manual effort.
  • Soil Moisture Sensor
    Detects the moisture content in soil. Sends analog signals to Arduino to determine if watering is needed.
  • Relay Module
    Acts as a switch controlled by Arduino to turn the water pump on or off based on sensor readings.
  • Water Pump
    Delivers water to the plant when powered by the relay module.

Procedure:

  1. Place the soil moisture sensor in the plant pot.
  2. Connect the sensor’s analog output to A0 pin of Arduino Uno.
  3. Connect the relay module’s IN pin to digital pin 8 of Arduino.
  4. Connect the relay's output to the water pump (through external power).
  5. Upload the Arduino code to the Uno board.
  6. Power the system using a USB cable or battery.
